Oil Analysis — March 16, 2026

🛢️ OIL MARKET DEEP DIVE — March 16, 2026

THE BIG PICTURE

US-Israel launched Operation Epic Fury against Iran on Feb 28. The Strait of Hormuz — carrying 20% of global oil supply — is effectively closed. The IEA called it the largest supply disruption in history. Gulf producers are shutting in fields because storage is full and tankers can't move through the strait.

SUPPLY DISRUPTION SCALE

  • ~17-20 million bbl/day of transit capacity offline through Hormuz
  • Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, and Qatar all impacted
  • Iraq facing acute onshore storage crisis — fields approaching shut-in
  • Alternative routes (Yanbu pipeline, Fujairah bypass) only cover ~5-6 million bbl/day max
  • Net disruption estimate: 11-14 million bbl/day — unprecedented in modern oil markets

PRICE ACTION

  • Brent: $89.74 — still remarkably contained given the disruption scale
  • WTI: $85.58 — US-centric pricing partially insulated
  • Markets pricing in diplomatic resolution within weeks — if that fails, expect a violent repricing to $107-119 Brent
  • The $87-92 range is the "ceasefire hope" floor — below that only on confirmed de-escalation

GEOPOLITICAL VARIABLES

  • Iran retaliation risk — missile strikes on Gulf oil infrastructure would send Brent parabolic
  • Trump-Putin diplomatic channel — any signals of mediation could cap upside
  • OPEC response: emergency meeting possible if disruption extends past April 5 scheduled meeting
  • SPR release — US has ~350M barrels, could deploy 1-2M bbl/day for months
  • China's strategic reserve drawdown — potential wildcard on demand side

BASE CASE SCENARIO

Hormuz remains disrupted for 2-4 more weeks. Brent trades $87-107 range. Ceasefire negotiations accelerate under economic pressure. OPEC+ holds emergency coordination. If ceasefire achieved by mid-April, Brent retraces to $80-85. If conflict escalates or Iranian retaliation materializes, $119-130 is in play.

THESIS

The market is underpricing the duration risk. Every day Hormuz stays closed, the storage math gets worse. Long Brent on dips to $87-92 with stops at $82. Take profit in tranches at $100, $107, $119. Flip short only on confirmed diplomatic breakthrough.
