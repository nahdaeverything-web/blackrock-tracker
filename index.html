Oil Analysis — March 22, 2026

🛢️ OIL MARKET DEEP DIVE — March 22, 2026

THE BIG PICTURE

Three weeks into the US-Israel operation against Iran, the conflict is entering a dangerous new phase. IDF struck Iran's South Pars gas facility (world's largest gas reserve) on Mar 18. Iran is threatening retaliatory strikes on Saudi, UAE, and Qatari energy infrastructure. Goldman Sachs now forecasts Brent above $100 for years if Hormuz pressure persists. Standard Chartered: "No clear off-ramps in the conflict."

SUPPLY DISRUPTION SCALE

  • ~17-20 million bbl/day of Hormuz transit capacity at risk
  • South Pars strike threatens Iran's gas exports — world's largest reserve now a target
  • Alternative routes (Yanbu, Fujairah) cover only ~5-6 million bbl/day
  • Gulf producers on high alert for retaliatory Iranian strikes on their facilities
  • Net disruption risk: 11-14 million bbl/day if retaliation materializes

PRICE ACTION (Mar 21)

  • Brent: $106.41 — off recent highs as US-Israel attempt to ease concerns
  • WTI: $98.23 — holding near $100 psychological level
  • Pullback is diplomatic optics, not de-escalation — no ceasefire signal
  • Goldman: $100+ floor could hold for a decade under sustained Hormuz pressure
  • Dips to $100-104 Brent are buying opportunities, not reversals

GEOPOLITICAL VARIABLES

  • 🔴 CRITICAL: Iran retaliation threat — strikes on Saudi/UAE/Qatar = $130+ immediate trigger
  • Trump managing narrative — trying to control price optics but no structural de-escalation
  • Russia re-entering the picture as diplomatic/supply wildcard
  • TACO trade being priced in by some — analysts warn of "rude awakening"
  • SPR deployment possible but insufficient against 11-14M bbl/day disruption

BASE CASE SCENARIO (Updated Mar 21)

Brent consolidates $100-112 range near-term as diplomatic noise caps upside. No ceasefire — conflict remains active. If Iran strikes Gulf energy infrastructure, $130+ in play immediately. If genuine de-escalation signals emerge, retracement to $90-95 possible. Goldman's decade-long $100+ forecast sets the structural floor.

THESIS

Stay long Brent. The war premium is structural, not speculative — Goldman agrees. Dips to $100-104 are entries, not exits. Stop at $95 (confirmed ceasefire only). Target $120-130 on Gulf facility strike or Hormuz full closure. The single biggest risk to the long is a surprise Trump-Iran back-channel deal — monitor for any diplomatic signals.
